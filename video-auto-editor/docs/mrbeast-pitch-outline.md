# MrBeast-Team Pitch Outline (Editor Positioning)

Date: 2026-03-15
Purpose: position editing as measurable retention engineering, not subjective aesthetics.

## 1) Positioning Statement

I deliver Shorts edits as a repeatable retention system:

- fast hook engineering
- evidence-first scene sequencing
- frame-accurate caption emphasis
- analytics-driven iteration on the first 3 seconds

I do not sell "cool transitions." I sell watch-time performance under strict quality control.

## 2) What Makes This Different From Typical Editors

Most editors provide one polished cut.
I provide:

- 3 hook variants for the same script
- one baseline and one aggressive pacing version
- a retention hypothesis for each version
- QA report against objective pre-publish gates

This lowers creative risk and accelerates channel learning.

## 3) Offer Structure

Per short deliverables:

- Version A: control edit
- Version B: hook-optimized edit
- Version C: pacing-optimized edit
- Caption strategy variants for first 5 seconds
- One-page post-edit rationale

Turnaround model:

- first-pass delivery
- one focused revision window
- final masters and project handoff

## 4) Quality Standard (Client-Facing)

All deliveries pass these gates:

- Hook Performance Gate:
  - first visual event <=0.8s
  - 3+ visual events in first 3s
- Caption Precision Gate:
  - emphasis words highlighted
  - no key subject occlusion
  - tight word sync
- Narrative Velocity Gate:
  - no dead visual spans
  - each cut has a narrative reason
- Delivery Gate:
  - clean audio hierarchy
  - vertical platform-safe export

## 5) How You Discuss Results In A Call

Use language like:

- "We increased early novelty density in the first 2 seconds."
- "We moved from descriptive sequencing to proof sequencing."
- "We separated comprehension captions from emphasis captions."
- "We tested hook variants to reduce dependence on one creative guess."

This signals operator-level thinking, not freelancer-level execution.

## 6) Sample 60-Second Workflow (What You Promise)

- Step 1: script beat mapping and risk flags
- Step 2: hook triad generation (3 opening versions)
- Step 3: scene-proof assembly and cadence pass
- Step 4: caption emphasis pass (word-level)
- Step 5: audio hierarchy pass (VO/music/SFX)
- Step 6: export and QA checklist

## 7) Negotiation Angle (Competitive Advantage)

Instead of competing on price per video:

- sell reliability, speed, and measurable process
- sell reduced iteration waste for the team
- sell better first-24-hour performance probability

Core message:

I am not another editor in the race. I am a short-form retention partner with a production system.
